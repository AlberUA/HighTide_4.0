Pembroke Pines, And a Warning To Florida’s Future

Pembroke Pines, which reaches the top of our list, is not on the coast at all. It borders the Everglades and is connected to the coast through a series of waterways. Even without the sandy beaches, Pembroke Pines will feel the effects of sea level rise as the water in the intricate pattern of canals rises with the changing patterns of the ocean.

Most of the press about sea level rising focuses on coastal towns. However, any low-lying area connected to the water can be affected. Whether that is through groundwater, man made canals, or other waterways, these cities need to take action as if they were directly on the beach.

Pembroke Pines and others must act now to elevate and restructure for the future well-being of their communities. However, the best adaptation strategies remain unclear without detailed cost-benefit analysis of all the options. Incentives for governments and individual property owners often become muddy, and differences in real estate values raise questions about equity in adaptation.
